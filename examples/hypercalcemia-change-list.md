## Editorial Review

### Derogatory or improper terms
- None found.

### Incorrect or inaccurate statements
1. "chicken, or fish" listed as calcium-rich foods — inaccurate; chicken and fish are not significant calcium sources. **Removed from list.**
2. "carrots, cucumbers and brown rice" suggested as alternatives — misleading; these are not calcium-free. **Removed from suggestion.**
3. "cardiovascular inflammation" in FAQ 1 — hypercalcemia causes arrhythmias and hypertension, not primarily "inflammation". **Corrected to "disturb heart rhythm".**
4. "remembering things for a longer duration" in FAQ 2 — should be "difficulty remembering" or "memory impairment". **Corrected.**
5. "renal inflammation" in FAQ 3 — more accurately "chronic kidney disease". **Corrected.**
6. "lithium increases the production of parathyroid hormones" — lithium shifts the calcium-sensing receptor set-point, it doesn't directly "increase production". **Corrected.**
7. "thiazide diuretics affect the renal system's ability to remove excess calcium" — thiazides reduce urinary calcium excretion by enhancing distal tubular reabsorption. **Corrected.**

### Repeated statements
- No repeated sentences. `[IMAGE: TBD]` appears twice intentionally at distinct insert points.

### Grammar / style notes (not edits)
- "hypercalcaemia" vs "hypercalcemia" — British spelling appears once in the additional tests section. Standardized to US spelling throughout.
- "milk alkali syndrome" — standard medical term is "milk-alkali syndrome" (hyphenated). **Corrected.**
